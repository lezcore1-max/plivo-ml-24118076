Why This Configuration

ChangeReasonExpected impact
BPE vocab=1500 Devanagari chars are 3 UTF-8 bytes each. Byte tokenizer produced 7.3M tokens from 7.3MB corpus (1.0 tok/byte). BPE gives 0.370 tok/byte — 2.7× compression — so each 128-token context window covers 2.7× more semantic content for Hindi text. Largest single improvement
Cosine LR 3e-3→3e-4, warmup=100 Baseline used constant 3e-4 with no warmup. High LR early causes large gradient steps before the model has meaningful representations; cosine decay allows aggressive early learning then fine-tuning. Second largest expected improvement
AdamW wd=0.1 (matrices only) Adam applies weight decay to all params including biases and layer norm scales which is incorrect. AdamW decouples decay and applies it only to weight matrices, providing mild regularization without distorting scale parameters. Moderate improvement
Grad clip=1.0 Needed when using 10× higher peak LR (3e-3 vs 3e-4). Without clipping, occasional large gradient spikes corrupt weights early in training. Stability, not accuracy
GPT-2 init (std=0.02, proj /√(2L)) Baseline std=0.05 is too large for proj layers; residual stream variance grows with depth. Scaling proj weights by 1/√(2*n_layers)=1/√8 keeps residual variance ≈1 at init regardless of depth. Moderate, especially early training
tie_weights=True Head and tok_emb share the same weight matrix. Saves vocab×embd=240,000 parameters and creates a useful gradient path: the embedding learns from both prediction loss and language modeling objectives simultaneously. Minor, plus parameter efficiency
BPE vocab=1500 (not 3072) Devanagari merges saturate by ~merge 600. Merges 601–2816 mostly add English subwords with diminishing returns at this model size. Cutting to 1500 saved ~17 min of BPE training time on a laptop CPU. Time tradeoff, not quality
